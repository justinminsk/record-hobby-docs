# Speaker Buying Guide

The golden rule of hi-fi audio is that **speakers make the biggest difference in your system's sound**. You can spend thousands on a turntable, but if you plug it into a cheap Bluetooth speaker, it will sound like a cheap Bluetooth speaker.

When buying speakers for a turntable, you have to make one massive decision right at the start: **Powered** or **Passive**.

---

## 1. Powered Speakers (Active)

Powered speakers have the stereo amplifier built directly into the cabinet. This means they plug directly into the wall for power, and you can plug your turntable directly into the back of them (assuming your turntable has a built-in phono preamp).

**Pros:**
- Much simpler setup with far fewer wires.
- Saves space (no need for a bulky stereo receiver).
- Generally cheaper to get started.

**Cons:**
- If the internal amplifier dies, the entire speaker system is useless.
- Harder to upgrade (you can't upgrade the amp later; you have to replace the whole speaker).

### Top Powered Recommendations
- **Budget (~$120): [Edifier R1280T](https://edifier-online.com/us/en/speakers/r1280t-powered-bookshelf-speakers)**
  The undisputed king of entry-level audio. They look great, have a warm sound, and are practically designed to be a beginner's first turntable speaker.
- **Mid-Range (~$300): [Klipsch R-41PM](https://www.klipsch.com/products/r-41pm-powered-speakers)**
  Klipsch is famous for their horn tweeters, providing incredibly dynamic, punchy sound. Crucially, these have a built-in phono preamp, meaning you can plug *any* turntable directly into them.
- **High-End (~$800): [Kanto TUK](https://www.kantoaudio.com/powered-speakers/tuk/)**
  Audiophile-grade powered speakers. They feature AMT ribbon tweeters for extreme detail, a built-in phono preamp, a dedicated subwoofer output, and a gorgeous minimalist design.

---

## 2. Passive Speakers

Passive speakers do *not* plug into the wall. They have no internal power. They require a separate stereo receiver or integrated amplifier to drive them via speaker wire.

**Pros:**
- Completely upgradeable (if you want more power later, buy a bigger amp; the speakers stay).
- Much wider selection of truly high-end audiophile options.
- Better value for pure sound quality once you get past the entry-level budget.

**Cons:**
- Requires more physical space (to house the amp/receiver).
- You have to learn how to strip and connect raw speaker wire.

### Top Passive Recommendations
- **Budget (~$150): [Sony SS-CS5](https://electronics.sony.com/audio/speakers/home-theater-speakers/p/sscs5)**
  These 3-way bookshelf speakers are widely considered the best value in passive audio under $200. They are incredibly clear and precise.
- **Mid-Range (~$350): [Triangle Borea BR03](https://www.trianglehifi.com/en/produit/hifi-bookshelf-speaker-borea-br03/)**
  Beautifully designed French speakers. They offer a very detailed, energetic sound that pairs wonderfully with jazz and rock records. 
- **High-End (~$350-$600): [KEF Q150](https://us.kef.com/products/q150-bookshelf-speaker)** *(Note: Often on sale)*
  Famous for their proprietary "Uni-Q" driver, where the tweeter sits perfectly inside the center of the woofer. This design creates a massive, perfect 3D soundstage. If you close your eyes, you won't be able to point to where the speakers are.
