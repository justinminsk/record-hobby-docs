# Example Setups by Budget

It is incredibly easy to get overwhelmed by all the possible combinations of turntables, preamps, receivers, and speakers. If you want to bypass the research and just buy a system that is guaranteed to sound great for the price, here are three proven templates.

*Note: Prices are rough estimates and fluctuate based on sales and availability.*

## 1. The $300 Starter (The Minimum Viable Setup)

This is the absolute cheapest you can go while guaranteeing you will not damage your records. It is a "Path A" setup (Turntable + Powered Speakers).

- **Turntable:** **[Audio-Technica AT-LP60X](https://www.audio-technica.com/en-us/at-lp60x)** (~$150)
  - *Why:* It's fully automatic (you press a button and the needle drops itself) and has a built-in phono preamp. It does not have an adjustable counterweight, but the factory sets it to a safe tracking force.
- **Speakers:** **[Edifier R1280T Powered Speakers](https://edifier-online.com/us/en/speakers/r1280t-powered-bookshelf-speakers)** (~$120)
  - *Why:* Because they are powered, you can plug the turntable's RCA cables directly into the back of them. They provide best-in-class sound for under $150.

**How it connects:** `AT-LP60X (Preamp ON)` ➔ RCA Cable ➔ `Edifier R1280T`

---

## 2. The $600 Sweet Spot (The Best Value)

This setup provides a massive leap in audio quality and introduces a fully upgradeable "Path C" system (Turntable + Receiver + Passive Speakers).

- **Turntable:** **[Fluance RT82](https://www.fluance.com/rt82w-reference-high-fidelity-vinyl-turntable-natural-walnut)** (~$300)
  - *Why:* Features a heavy MDF plinth, an adjustable tonearm, an optical speed sensor, and a great Ortofon OM10 cartridge. *(Note: It does not have a built-in preamp).*
- **Receiver:** **[Sony STR-DH190](https://electronics.sony.com/audio/audio-components/receivers/p/strdh190)** (~$150)
  - *Why:* A bulletproof entry-level stereo receiver that includes a dedicated "Phono" input. This solves the preamp issue!
- **Speakers:** **[Sony SS-CS5 Passive Speakers](https://electronics.sony.com/audio/speakers/home-theater-speakers/p/sscs5)** (~$150)
  - *Why:* Universally praised passive speakers that punch way above their price point.

**How it connects:** `Fluance RT82` ➔ RCA Cable (w/ Ground Wire) ➔ `Sony Receiver (Phono In)` ➔ Speaker Wire ➔ `Sony SS-CS5`

---

## 3. The $1,200 Audiophile Entry

For those who want true high-fidelity sound, gorgeous aesthetics, and a massive 3D soundstage right out of the gate.

- **Turntable:** **[Pro-Ject Debut Carbon EVO](https://pro-jectusa.com/product/debut-carbon-evo/)** (~$600)
  - *Why:* A gorgeous, minimalist turntable with a carbon-fiber tonearm and an excellent Sumiko Rainier cartridge pre-installed.
- **Amplifier:** **[Yamaha A-S301](https://usa.yamaha.com/products/audio_visual/hifi_components/a-s301/index.html)** (~$350)
  - *Why:* A beautiful, vintage-looking integrated amp with an incredibly clean, high-quality built-in phono stage.
- **Speakers:** **[KEF Q150](https://us.kef.com/products/q150-bookshelf-speaker)** (Wait for sales: ~$350) or **[Triangle Borea BR03](https://www.trianglehifi.com/en/produit/hifi-bookshelf-speaker-borea-br03/)**
  - *Why:* These are legendary entry-level audiophile speakers that provide incredible detail and imaging.

**How it connects:** `Pro-Ject EVO` ➔ RCA Cable (w/ Ground Wire) ➔ `Yamaha Amp (Phono In)` ➔ Speaker Wire ➔ `KEF Q150`
