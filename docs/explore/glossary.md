# Glossary of Terms

The vinyl hobby is full of jargon. Here is a quick reference guide to help you translate forums, Reddit threads, and product pages.

## Equipment

- **Tonearm:** The arm that holds the cartridge and swings across the record.
- **Cartridge:** The small block at the end of the tonearm containing the stylus and the electromagnetic system that translates vibrations into an electrical signal.
- **Stylus:** The needle itself (usually a microscopic diamond) attached to a tiny metal tube called the cantilever.
- **Platter:** The heavy, rotating disc that the record sits on.
- **Plinth:** The base or chassis of the turntable. A heavier, denser plinth resists vibration better.
- **Headshell:** The piece at the very end of the tonearm that the cartridge screws into. Some tonearms have removable headshells for easy cartridge swapping.
- **Phono Preamp (Phono Stage):** An electronic circuit that applies the RIAA equalization curve to the signal and boosts the tiny voltage from the cartridge to a standard "Line Level" voltage that an amplifier can use.

## Cartridge Types

- **MM (Moving Magnet):** The most common cartridge type. They output a relatively high signal and usually allow you to replace the stylus yourself when it wears out.
- **MC (Moving Coil):** Typically found on high-end setups. They output a much lower signal (requiring a specialized preamp) and the stylus cannot be replaced by the user—the whole cartridge must be traded in or retipped.

## Settings & Measurements

- **Tracking Force (VTF):** The amount of downward pressure the stylus puts on the record. Set by adjusting the counterweight at the back of the tonearm.
- **Anti-Skate:** A mechanism (usually a small dial or a dangling weight) that pulls the tonearm slightly outward to counteract the natural inward pull generated by the spinning record, keeping the needle perfectly centered in the groove.
- **Wow and Flutter:** A measurement of speed instability. "Wow" is a slow fluctuation in pitch, and "Flutter" is a fast fluctuation. Lower numbers are better.
- **VTA (Vertical Tracking Angle):** The angle at which the cantilever enters the record. Usually adjusted by raising or lowering the pivot point of the entire tonearm.

## The Record

- **Groove:** The continuous spiral trench pressed into the vinyl. The left and right walls of the groove contain the left and right audio channels.
- **Run-in Groove:** The smooth outer edge of the record that catches the needle before the music starts.
- **Run-out Groove / Deadwax:** The smooth inner section between the end of the music and the label.
- **Matrix Numbers:** The letters and numbers etched or stamped into the deadwax. Collectors use these to identify the exact pressing plant, mastering engineer, and release version of a record.
- **45 RPM vs 33 ⅓ RPM:** The speed at which the record must spin. 12-inch albums usually play at 33 ⅓ RPM, while 7-inch singles play at 45 RPM (though some audiophile albums are spread across multiple 12-inch discs cut at 45 RPM for better sound quality).
