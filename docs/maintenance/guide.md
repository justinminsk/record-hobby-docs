# Maintenance Guide

Once your setup is in place, keeping it sounding good requires very little effort if you build a few simple habits.

## The Routine

### Before Every Play
1. **Brush the record:** Lightly hold your carbon-fiber brush against the spinning record for a few rotations, then sweep the dust off the edge. Do not press hard.
2. **Check the stylus:** If you see dust or lint caught on the needle, gently clean it (see below).

### When Buying Used Records
- **Wet clean before playing:** Even if a record looks clean, it often has dust deep in the grooves. Playing a dirty record grinds that dirt into the vinyl and wears out your stylus faster.
- **Replace the inner sleeve:** Old paper sleeves act like sandpaper and hold onto dust. Swap them for anti-static poly-lined sleeves.

## Cleaning the Stylus

The stylus (needle) is delicate, so always be gentle and only clean it front-to-back, never side-to-side.

- **Using a stylus brush:** Brush gently from the back of the stylus toward the front.
- **Using cleaning gel (like Onzow ZeroDust):** Lower the tonearm so the stylus dips straight down into the gel, then lift it straight up. Do not twist or pull the tonearm while it's in the gel.

## Long-Term Maintenance

### Stylus Replacement
A diamond stylus typically lasts between 300 to 1,000 hours of play, depending on the profile (elliptical, microlinear, etc.) and how clean your records are.

- **Signs you need a new stylus:** The sound becomes distorted, particularly in the inner grooves (closest to the label), or high frequencies sound fuzzy and harsh.
- **The fix:** For most moving magnet (MM) cartridges, you can just pull the old stylus off and snap a new one on. You don't need to replace the entire cartridge.

### Turntable Belt
If you have a belt-drive turntable, the rubber belt will eventually stretch out or dry rot, usually after a few years.

- **Signs you need a new belt:** The speed fluctuates, the pitch sounds wavery (wow and flutter), or the turntable takes a long time to get up to speed.
- **The fix:** Buy a replacement belt designed specifically for your turntable model.
